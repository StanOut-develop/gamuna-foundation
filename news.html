DWU Students, Student Rep Council & Gamuna Foundation Team Up for Community Outreach

Students from Divine Word University, the Student Representative Council, and the Gamuna Foundation joined forces on a donation drive and awareness programme for local communities. Founder Nicholas Gamuna spoke on the importance of respecting laws and keeping communities informed of civic responsibilities, with the group committing to continue awareness campaigns focused on the challenges facing today's youth.

Feature Story May 2026

The Story Behind the Gamuna Foundation

A feature piece traced the foundation's roots to founder Nicholas Gamuna's final year studying Social and Religious Studies at Divine Word University, and his time as DWU Madang Students Association President in 2025, where he saw firsthand the challenges facing local youth. The foundation is built on the idea of Holistic Human Development — nurturing people's physical, emotional, social, and spiritual wellbeing — and runs programmes spanning awareness campaigns on gender-based violence, law and order, health and education, plus a youth-led community policing initiative in the Sumkar District. The piece also noted a Donation Drive running from May 9th through the end of the month.

Media Coverage February 2026

Gamuna Foundation Visits Beon Prison, Madang

Members of the Gamuna Foundation visited inmates at Beon Prison in Madang, carrying out awareness activities and donating clothes, food and drinks. Founder Nicholas Gamuna said the prison was chosen so youth in the programme could witness firsthand the consequences of crime, engage with inmates, and share messages of hope — reinforcing that there is always an opportunity to change and become a better person after prison.
